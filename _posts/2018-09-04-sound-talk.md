## Localisation

Changing the shape of covering your ear can make localisation very difficult to get to.
Sound from the front arrives equal level and both ears. Sounds from the side id reflected and shadowed by the head.
This is called shadowing, and is less obvious to localise with lower frequencies

The shape of the hear is where height localisation comes, which changes the sound as it moves into the ear. Sounds that come from the top, and around a higher part of the pinna. These small differences only appear in the higher frequencies.
