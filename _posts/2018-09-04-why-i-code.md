Before mentioning the cliche's of why you should code - problem solving, logic, integration and organisational skills, I probably ended up as a programmer from the creative freedom it gives.

Whilst studying as a masters student at the University of York in computer science, I learned that being a good developer is not about being able to list a tonne of languages you know, or recite an entire API, although these things do help. For me, it was knowing what was already there in front of you.

I never discovered creative freedom by coding - and I'm glad I didn't. It was from the early adoption of piano and clarinet, that lead to a degree at Sussex in music... notably in composition. Music gave


Problem solving
Communication and Teamwork
Developmental skills
STEM and Arts integration
Organizational skills
Lifelong Skills
…and most importantly: unlimited creative freedom
